He paused to look round before entering.  A churchyard. Here, then; the wretched man whose name he had now to    learn, lay underneath the ground. It was a worthy place. Walled in by houses; overrun by gra ss and weeds, the growth of vegetation’s death, not life; choked up with too much burying; fat with repleted appetite. A worthy place!  The Spirit stood among the graves, and pointed down to One. He advanced towards it trembling. The Phantom was exactly as it had been, but he dreaded that he saw new meaning in its solemn shape.  “Before I draw nearer to that stone to which you point,” said Scrooge, “answer me one question.
